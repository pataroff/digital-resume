Pre-Development

'Innotopia' is the new brand identity name of 'Indietopia'.

Throughout the years, 'Indietopia' shifted its activities from hosting an accelerator program towards creating interactive experiences and gamified solutions for businesses and cultural institutions. To support these changes, the team of 'Indietopia' thought it was a good time for a new identity and a new website.

Therefore, after I did my internship at the company and hearing the plans about the rebrand, I proposed to them to do their new website.

I had been learning web development for more than half a year by then and I thought this would be a good opportunity to build a project on my own and see where I stand.

Design

Before I started with the development, I decided to first create the design for the website. I figured out that having a reference would make it easier to code the layout instead of designing it on the spot.

Additionally, I wanted to first get approval on the design from 'Indietopia' before starting to write any code, as it would be much easier to make changes in the design phase than in the development phase.

Therefore, I made a quick sketch of my design idea to pitch it to the 'Indietopia' team.

After pitching my idea, the 'Indietopia' team approved it, and it seemed like we were all on the same page.

With their feedback and input, I refined the design further by iterating on it until it aligned with their vision and objectives.

This collaborative process not only strengthened the design but also fostered a sense of shared ownership of the project.

In this first iteration, I was trying to add some color to the original design as some of the team members found it to be too blank.

The project manager suggested 'lime green' and I decided to experiment with it.

In the end, it didn't look very well as the primary color, but I decided to still keep it as a possible accent color in order to adhere to the design requirements of the client.

Innotopia Website v3:

Hover over the image and scroll down.

In this second iteration, I was still trying to find the 'right color' for the design of the website while also making some adjustments to the different sections.

There were suggestions for 'purple' and different shades of it, but in the end we concluded that the color is overused on the web nowadays and decided to drop it out.

Additionaly, I thought it would be a good idea to have a 'hero' image that represents the brand identity of 'Indietopia'.

Considering the fact that 'Indietopia' is based in Groningen and most of their business partnerships are with other local comapnies, I thought it would be fitting to include something that represent the Groningen area.

Brent Keuning came up with this 'hexagonal tile' that includes 2 of the most popular buildings in the Groningen area - 'Martini Tower' and 'Forum Groningen'.

Innotopia Website v4:

Hover over the image and scroll down.

In this third iteration, I finally settled on a 'dark blue' as the primary color for the website and finalized the layout of the different sections.

This iteration resonated most strongly with the 'Indietopia' team. The choice of 'dark blue' and the refined layout perfectly captured the essence of their brand, evoking a sense of professionalism and trustworthiness that aligned well with their goals and vision.

From this point onward, the design remained mostly unchanged, with only minor adjustments to the colors.

The initial design requirement for 'lime green' was met by using it as an accent color in the final version of the design.
